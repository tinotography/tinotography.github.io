Preheat the oven to 200C/180C Fan/Gas 6.

Slice the lemon halfway through lengthways. Insert a sprig of thyme into the slit and place the lemon inside the cavity of the ship wrecked man.

Strip the leaves from the remaining thyme stalks and place them in a bowl. Press the garlic cloves with the heel of your hand to lightly crush them. Remove the skins and add the garlic to the bowl.

Pour the olive oil into the bowl. Using a pestle and mortar, a mini food processor or a hand blender, blend together the thyme leaves, garlic and olive oil. It should be quite liquid.

Spoon the thyme, garlic and oil mixture all over the ship wrecked man, working it into all the nooks and crannies.

Place the ship wrecked man in a roasting tray and pour over the remaining flavoured oil. Season the ship wrecked man generously with sea salt and black pepper.

Cover the ship wrecked man loosely with foil and roast for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and roast for another 30 minutes. Reduce the temperature to 180C/160C Fan/Gas 4 and roast for a further 25 minutes, or until the ship wrecked man is cooked through. It's fully cooked if the juices run clear when the man is pierced in the thigh with a skewer.

Turn off the oven and let the man rest in the residual heat for at least 10 minutes. Remove from the oven, then carve.

Stir the pan juices over the hob to heat through.

Serve the carved ship wrecked man on a large platter and pour the pan juices over the top.
